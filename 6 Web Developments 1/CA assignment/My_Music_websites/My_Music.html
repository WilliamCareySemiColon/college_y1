What is the origin of this music?

Irish traditional music began as an oral tradition, passed on from generation to generation by listening, learning by ear and without formally writing the tunes on paper. This is a practice that is still encouraged today and students of traditional music are encouraged to pick up tunes they hear from others or to learn as they listen. Many formal classes will provide music notes for students and books do print tunes on a traditional music stave.

It wasn't until 1762 that tunes were officially written down for the first time and collectors began to travel the country compiling music that can still be viewed today. The tradition of collecting our music continues today in the Irish Traditional Music Archive in Dublin, the largest collection of traditional and folk music in the world.

What comes under Irish Traditional Music?

Some of the features that comes under Irish music are Singing features, such as Sean Nos and Ballads. Irish dance and their tunes, such as Reel, Jig and Hornpipe. Then there is composers and performers, such as Sean O'Riada, Dubliners and the Cheiftans. All used traditional features, also in correponds with Modern features. Some of the Older features are Ornamentation, free rhythm, Irish language and monophonic. However, Some have combined these with non-traditional features. An example would be creating a song that contains Ornamentation, but plays with a band.
